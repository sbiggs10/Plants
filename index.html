Plants that Grow Well in Texas

Lamb's Ear

Lamb's Ear is a very resilient plant that can survive extreme heat and very dry conditions. It thrives in both direct sunlight and partial shade. Lamb's Ear makes exellent ground cover, given the fact that it is very invassive. Lamb's Ear will produce 12 to 18 inch tall flowers in the spring. These flowers are light purple.

Texas Mountain Laurel

Texas Mountain Laurel is native to Mexico and the Southwestern United States. It is a small tree or shrub that produces beautiful purple flowers, which smell like grape soda. The seeds produced by this plant are toxic to animals and humans.

Lantana

Lantana plants have clusters of flowers that can be a mix of red, orange, yellow, pink, blue, or white.These flowwers are very invasive.
