Lamb's Ear

Lamb's Ear is a very resilient plant that can survive extreme heat and very dry conditions. It thrives in both direct sunlight and partial shade. Lamb's Ear makes exellent ground cover, given the fact that it is very invassive. Lamb's Ear will produce 12 to 18 inch tall flowers in the spring. These flowers are light purple.

Texas Mountain Laurel

Texas Mountain Laurel is native to Mexico and the Southwestern United States. It is a small tree or shrub that produces beautiful purple flowers, which smell like grape soda. The seeds produced by this plant are toxic to animals and humans.

Lantana

Lantana plants have clusters of flowers that can be a mix of red, orange, yellow, pink, blue, or white.These flowwers are very invasive.
Plant Care/Facts
Plant Sun Requirements ☀ Water Requirements ☂ Uses
Lamb's Ear Full sun to partial shade Water regularly until plant is established, then reduce the frequency of watering. Lamb's Ear is susceptible to root rot if over watered. Lamb's can be used for the treatment of infections, wounds, common cold, etc.
Texas Mountain Laurel Full sun to part shade Water every 3-4 days for the first few weeks. Then water every 4-7 days for the first years. After the first year Texas Mountain Laurel should be watered once a week in the summner, every 2-3 weeks in the spring, and once a month in the fall and winter. It can be used to treat ringworm of the scalp (tinea capitis), psoriasis, herpes, and syphilis.
Lantane Full sun Needs to be watered regularly Lantane plant leave contain antimicrobial, fungicidal, and insecticidal prperties.
Cephalocereus senilis Full sun Water once a week. It prefers a very well-drained soil mix. Landscape accent
